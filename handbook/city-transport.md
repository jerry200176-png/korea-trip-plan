# 城市交通

**Sources:** T-money (`src-tmoney`), Korail (`src-korail`), Naver Map (`src-naver-map`), Kakao Map (`src-kakao-map`).

## Principles for this couple

- No rental car.
- Prefer **one primary area per day** (max two adjacent).
- Cap transfers; upgrade to taxi when walking/transfer load hurts feet.
- Long-distance day (KTX) must stay light on sightseeing.

## T-money / transit cards

- T-money is widely used for metro/bus. Purchase/top-up options change — follow [t-money.co.kr](https://www.t-money.co.kr) and convenience-store instructions on site.
- Mobile options may exist depending on phone/region; verify compatibility for your iPhones before relying on them.

## Metro / bus

- Use **Naver Map** or **Kakao Map** for live routes, exits, and walking segments.
- Watch last-train times nightly (especially Day 1 and transfer days).

## KTX (if Seoul + Busan)

- Book via official Korail / Let’s Korail (`src-korail`) after route lock.
- Sit together; store luggage carefully; avoid overpacking the transfer day.

## Taxi

- Useful for luggage, rain, late night, or foot fatigue.
- Keep lodging address in Korean in Emergency Pack.

## Elevators, lockers, large bags

- Not all metro transfers are luggage-friendly.
- Station lockers vary; do not plan critical itinerary around unverified lockers.
- On KTX day: leave hotel late enough to move bags without sprinting.
