# 城市交通

**Updated:** 2026-07-21 Transport Textbook  
**Sources:** T-money / VisitKorea transportation cards, Seoul Metro, AREX, Korail, Naver Map endpoints.

## Principles for this couple

- No rental car.
- Prefer **one primary area per day** (max two adjacent).
- Cap transfers; upgrade to taxi when walking/transfer load hurts feet.
- Long-distance day (KTX) must stay light on sightseeing.
- **Naver Map** primary; Kakao Map backup; Google Maps secondary for Korea transit.

## T-money / transit cards

- Buy and top up at convenience stores and station channels (confirm on VisitKorea / T-money operator pages nearer travel).
- Tap on entry and exit.
- Mobile wallet options depend on phone/region — verify both iPhones before relying on them.
- Low balance: top up; never jump gates.

## Metro / bus

- Use live maps for exits, elevators, and last trains **the night before**.
- Do not freeze a single last-train clock into this handbook.
- Luggage days: fewer transfers; elevators over long stair transfers when possible.

## Airport arrival (ICN teaching)

- Step order: immigration → baggage → customs → connectivity/cash/T-money → transfer.
- AREX Express vs All-Stop vs Airport Limousine vs taxi Rescue — see site `/transport/`.
- Limousine typically needs an **advance ticket** (cash/T-money not accepted for boarding per official tourism guide).
- Lodging not locked → no door-to-door minute claims.

## KTX (Seoul + Busan)

- Book via official Korail / Let’s Korail after dates allow.
- Until then: Date Pending — teach buffer, platform, luggage, missed-train recovery only.
- Sit together; keep the transfer day sightseeing-free.

## Busan local + Gimhae exit

- Metro / bus / taxi; coastal evening returns may need taxi Rescue.
- Gimhae exit has normal / luggage / low-energy versions; harden after flight time exists.

## Taxi

- Rescue for luggage, rain, late night, foot fatigue, last train gone, transfer failure.
- Keep lodging address in Korean in Emergency Pack.

## Failure recovery

- Wrong direction / missed stop: next station, reverse.
- Pair separation: stay at last agreed point; call; 112 if needed.
- Hard Stop examples: lost passport/documents; lost luggage requiring report; missed international connection you cannot rebook alone.

Full diagrams and recovery tables live on the public `/transport/` and `/emergency/` pages.
